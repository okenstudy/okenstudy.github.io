6. deskripsikan lebah dalam bahasa Inggris


Honey bees appear to originate from South and South East Asia, as all but one of the species are native to that region. Most species of honey bee have been cultured for their production of honey and beeswax, with only two species being truly domesticated.

There is only one documented fossil species of honey bee – ‘Apis Nearctica’; a single 14 million year old specimen found in Nevada.

There are no honey bees that are native to North or South America. European colonists brought the dark bee to the Americas, followed later by the introduction of Italian bees.

Africanized honey bees are better known colloquially as ‘killer bees’. They are hybrids between European stock and one of the African subspecies. They are more resistant to disease and are better foragers. Small swarms of Africanized honey bees take over European honey beehives by invading the hive, killing the queen and installing their own queen.

Two species of bee are used by beekeepers – ‘A. Mellifera’ and ‘A. Cerana’. They are maintained, fed and transported by the beekeepers from field to field as the crops need pollinating.

Colony Collapse Disorder (CCD) is the name given to the abnormally high die-offs of the European honey bee colonies. Research evidence suggests this is a ‘syndrome’, rather than a ‘disease’, as it is caused by a combination of factors – including impaired protein production in cells.

A colony of bees consists of only one queen bee, along with a few thousand fertile, male drone bees and a seasonally variable population of sterile, female worker bees.

The queen bee can choose whether to fertilize the egg she is laying – usually dependent on what cell she is laying the egg in. Unfertilized eggs develop into Drones, whilst fertilized eggs develop into female worker or Queen bees.

Larvae are initially fed with royal jelly. This is produced by worker bees and secreted from glands in their pharynx. Later this is switched to pollen and honey, except in the case of larvae destined to become Queen bees – they are fed on royal jelly throughout their growing life.

Young worker bees clean the hive and feed the larvae. As they get older they progress onto other within-colony tasks, including guarding the hive before eventually spending the rest of her life as a forager

10. definisi lebah dalan bahasa inggris


Bees are flying insects closely related to wasps and ants, known for their role in pollination and, in the case of the best-known bee species, the European honey bee, for producing honey and beeswax. Bees are a monophyletic lineage within the superfamily Apoidea, presently considered as a clade Anthophila. There are nearly 20,000 known species of bees in seven to nine recognized families,[1] though many are undescribed and the actual number is probably higher. They are found on every continent except Antarctica, in every habitat on the planet that contains insect-pollinated flowering plants.bees are flying insect. they make honey

15. mendeskripsikan hewan lebah ke dalam bahasa inggris beserta artinya




Honey Bee
Honey bee is the name given to some species of bees who have the ability to produce honey and beeswax. Of all 20.000 species of bees in the world, there are only seven species which belong to the group of honey bee. They are insect and all of them belong to the genus Apis. One of the most famous honey bee is the Western Honey Bee.
There are three castes of honey bee and each of them has their own job or function. The first one is the Queen. The queen is a female honey bee with the job to lay eggs and to be the mother of all bees in the colony. There is only one queen in a colony of bees. An exclusive food called "royal jelly" is what makes a female honey bee grow larger than other honey bees and became the queen. The second one is the Workers. The workers are all female honey bees in a colony except the queen. They can consist of as much as 60.000 bees in single colony. Some of their duties are: feeding the brood, collecting food, receiving nectar, cleaning the hive, guard duty, producing honey and also producing beeswax. The third one is the Drones. Drones are all male bees in a colony. The drones have one job only and that is to fertilize the queen during the mating process and will soon be dead after that.
The largest species of bee in the world is the Indonesian resin bee who can grow up to the size of 39 millimetres and the smallest species is the Dwarf stingless bees with the size of 2 millimetres. They have the typical body of an insect with three sections: the head, thorax and abdomen. They have two large eyes, two antenna, a mouth and a long proboscis on their head. They use the proboscis to suck up nectar from a flower which later be kept on their hind legs. There are three pairs of legs and two pairs of wings on their thorax. There are nine segments on their abdomen and the last three segments is modified into the sting.

Lebah Madu
Lebah madu adalah nama yang diberikan pada beberapa spesies lebah yang memiliki kemampuan untuk memproduksi madu dan lilin lebah. Dari keseluruhan 20.000 spesies lebah di dunia, hanya tujuh spesies yang termasuk kedalam kelompok lebah madu. Mereka adalah serangga dan mereka semua termasuk kedalam genus Apis. Satu lebah madu yang paling terkenal adalah Western Honey Bee.
Terdapat tiga kasta dari lebah madu dan masing masing dari mereka memiliki tugas atau fungsi mereka masing masing. Yang pertama adalah Ratu Lebah. Ratu lebah adalah lebah betina dengan tugas untuk bertelur dan menjadi induk dari semua lebah dalam koloni tersebut. Hanya ada satu ratu lebah dalam satu koloni lebah. Sebuah makanan ekslusip yang disebut "royal jelly" adalah yang membuat seekor lebah betina tumbuh lebih besar dari lebah lainnya dan menjadi ratu. Yang kedua adalah Lebah Pekerja. Lebah Pekerja adalah seluruh lebah betina dalam satu koloni kecuali sang ratu. Mereka bisa berjumlah 60.000 lebah dalam satu koloni. Beberapa tugas mereka adalah: memberi makan anak anak lebah.

18. jelaskan reproduksi lebah dalam bahasa inggris


Honey Bee

The life cycle begins when an established colony’s queen begins laying eggs within individual cells inside a honeycomb.

Queens store more than 5 million sperm cells inside their bodies, enabling them to lay eggs throughout their life after only one mating flight. When the eggs hatch, those that were fertilized become female worker bees, while the unfertilized eggs become male bees, or drones. It is the responsibility of the queen to lay enough fertilized eggs to produce a well-developed force of worker bees for the colony.

Bees pass through four stages: eggs, larvae, pupae and adults. Bee eggs measure approximately 1 mm long. Queen bees examine their eggs before placing them side by side at the center of the comb frame, with pollen surrounding them. Queens can lay up to 2,000 eggs each day throughout the spring. As queens age, the number of eggs they lays significantly diminishes. They may also no longer be able to place the eggs closely together, resulting in a patchy comb.

After three days, the eggs hatch into larvae, which have no eyes, wings, legs or antennae. Inside the hives, certain bees are responsible for feeding the larvae with a combination of pollen and honey. Around six days after hatching as larvae, they reach the third stage, spin cocoons and eventually hatch into adult bees after another seven to 10 days. Like ants, newly hatched bees have different designated responsibilities until they grow old.

25. ciri-ciri lebah dalam bahasa Inggris dan artinya​


BEE CHARACTERISTICS

Most bees have short, thick bodies covered with hair and, like all insects, six legs and three body parts: head, thorax, and abdomen. The thorax in turn has three segments, each with a pair of legs. A tiny waist connects the thorax and abdomen.

CIRI LEBAH

Sebagian besar lebah memiliki tubuh pendek dan tebal yang ditutupi rambut dan, seperti semua serangga, enam kaki dan tiga bagian tubuh: kepala, dada, dan perut. Toraks pada gilirannya memiliki tiga segmen, masing-masing dengan sepasang kaki. Memiliki pinggang mungil menghubungkan dada dan perut.

Once upon a time, in a dense jungle lived a hardworking bee named Benny. Benny was known for his strong work ethic and determination to gather as much nectar as possible for his hive. One day, while Benny was out gathering nectar, he came across a majestic elephant named Ellie. Ellie was intrigued by Benny and asked him what he was doing. Benny explained that he was gathering nectar to bring back to his hive. Ellie laughed and said that gathering nectar was a small task compared to the big things she did. Benny, determined to prove himself, challenged Ellie to a contest to see who could gather the most nectar in one day.

Ellie accepted the challenge and they set out into the jungle. Benny flew from flower to flower quickly gathering nectar while Ellie used her trunk to reach the higher flowers. As the day went on, Benny noticed that Ellie was struggling to keep up with his speed. Benny, being the kind bee that he was, offered to help Ellie gather nectar by showing her the best flowers to collect from. Together, they worked as a team and were able to gather more nectar than they ever could have alone.

At the end of the day, they both returned to their homes with a bountiful amount of nectar. Ellie was amazed by Benny's hard work and determination and thanked him for his help. Benny was grateful for Ellie's strength and size, which helped them gather even more nectar. They both realized that working together was much more efficient and effective than working alone.

From that day forward, Benny and Ellie became the best of friends. They would often be seen working together in the jungle, gathering nectar and helping each other out. The other animals in the jungle were amazed by their teamwork and began to learn from their example. Benny and Ellie's friendship and cooperation had a positive impact on the entire jungle community.

The moral of the story is that working together can accomplish more than working alone. Benny and Ellie, who were once strangers, became the best of friends and were able to achieve more by working together. They showed that by helping each other, they were able to achieve their goals and make their community a better place.
