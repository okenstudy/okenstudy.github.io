o Background

Forest fires are fires that occur in the wild, but can also destroy nearby homes and agricultural land. If a forest fire occurs, the fire will burn anything near it and spread in the direction of the wind. Fire occurs because of two things, including natural fires and human-caused fires. Initial forest fires were thought to occur naturally, even though in reality they caused forest fires, namely humans. Humans burn forests to facilitate hunting and open new land.

o Definition

According to the Minister of Forestry Regulation. Fire is a situation where the forest is hit by fire resulting in damage to forests and forest products that cause economic losses.

o Symptoms

Forest Fire is one of the annual disasters experienced by Indonesia. Forest fires generally occur in the dry season. As for the signs before a forest fire occurs, among others, the temperature around the forest gets hot. The wind that blows around the forest is hot and the humidity of the air decreases due to the long dry season.

o Type

There are 3 types of forest fires

1. Ground Fire

In this type, the fire spreads erratically because it is not affected by the wind. Fire burns organic material by combustion that does not ignite so only white smoke appears above the surface.

2. Surface Fire.

In this type, fire burns organic materials and vegetation. This fire spreads according to the direction of the wind because this fire is affected by the wind.

3. Crown Fire.

This fire is a major fire event and occurs as a result of spreading surface fires. However, this is not always the case, because crown fires occur separately, for example due to lightning strikes.  

o Impact

The impact of this forest fire is very much.

1. Forest fires cause the spread of smoke and carbon dioxide emissions that have an impact on global warming and climate change.

2. Reducing the source of clean water and causing drought, because forest fires cause loss of trees that store clean water reserves.

o Causes

In this case forest fires can occur naturally and human activities. Forest fires are naturally triggered by lightning and friction between trees. Lightning strikes and tree friction can turn into fires if the condition of the forest allows, such as a long drought. Whereas forest fires triggered by human activities can be caused by two things, intentionally and unintentionally. Deliberate fires are mostly caused by exploitation of natural resources. Whereas accidental fires are caused by negligence, such as burning garbage in the forest area.

o How to prevent

To prevent forest fires several attempts were made

1. During the dry season, you should not carelessly burn.

2. Don't burn or dispose of cigarette butts on grass, dry bushes and in locations that are prone to fire.

Both of these efforts can minimize the occurrence of forest fires .
