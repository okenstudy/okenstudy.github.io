┏━━━━━━✦ Explanation ✦ ━━━━━━━┓

Dalam kehidupan kita sehari-hari, diperlukan sebuah percakapan yang dibentuk antara berbagai pihak mulai dari pihak satu hingga pihak lainnya. Percakapan sendiri disebut sebagai komunikasi dan dapat dilakukan dengan dua cara yaitu lisan dan tertulis.

Percakapan adalah sebuah komunikasi yang dilakukan oleh pihak pembicara dengan pihak pendengar. Percakapan sendiri sering dilakukan dalam kehidupan di berbagai bahasa. Tanpa adanya percakapan, dunia akan sangat senyap.

◆━━━━━━━━ Type of Conversation ━━━━━━━━◆

Berikut adalah jenis percakapan dalam Bahasa Inggris :

Monologue : Percakapan yang dilakukan dengan diri sendiri

Dialogue : Percakapan yang dilakukan dengan dua orang.

Mr. Hendra, age 70 years old he is hospitalized due to chest pain.

Nurse: Did you sleep well last night?

Mr. Hendra: I tried to sleep early but I couldn’t, so I only got maybe 3 hours of sleep.

Nurse: Okay Mr. Hendra, I will check your pulse, temperature and blood pressure.

Mr. Hendra: Sure, please do.

Nurse: Well, as I see from the result everything is a bit higher than normal so I assume that this is what causing your chest pain and headache. I will let your doctor know so he can prescribe the proper medication for you.

Mr. Hendra: Thank you for your help nurse.

Nurse: No worries, sir. It’s my job.

23. Percakapan Perawat Dan Pasien Dalam Bahasa Inggris​


Jawaban:

Nurse : Perawat

Patient : Pasien

Nurse: Good morning! How are you feeling today?

Patient: Good morning, I'm feeling okay.

Nurse: That's good to hear. I'm here to take your vitals and give you your medications. Have you had any pain or discomfort since we last spoke?

Patient: No, I haven't had any pain.

Nurse: Great. Let me just take your blood pressure and temperature first.

(After taking vitals)

Nurse: Your blood pressure and temperature look good. Now, here are your medications. Do you have any questions about them?

Patient: Yes, actually. I noticed that one of the pills looks different than the others. Is that normal?

Nurse: Oh, let me check. That pill is a new medication that your doctor prescribed. It's okay to take it with your other medications.

Patient: Okay, thank you for explaining that to me.

Nurse: Of course. Is there anything else you need or any other concerns you have?

Patient: No, I think that's it for now.

Nurse: Alright then. Don't hesitate to call us if you need anything. Have a good day!

Patient: You too, thank you.

Patient: That's good to hear. Do you think there's anything wrong with my stomach?

Nurse: It's hard to say at this point. I will need to discuss your symptoms and test results with the doctor. But for now, just try to rest and let us take care of you.

Patient: Okay, thank you for your help.

Nurse: You're welcome. I'll be back later to check on you.
