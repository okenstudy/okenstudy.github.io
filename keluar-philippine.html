The solution? “We are trying to __37__ uses for tobacco,” says Perlita Baula of the National Tobacco Administration. Traditional healers in the Philippines use tobacco leaves __38__ wounds and insect bites, researchers want to assess the plant's potential as a topical pain killer and antibacterial medicine. The plant also has a high protein __39__, which can be extracted and added to food supplements. We can complete the text with following words:

The Philippine government knows that smoking is deadly. But it also knows that more than 60.000 farmers rely on tobacco (36) crops for a living. The solution? “We are trying to (37) find alternative uses for tobacco,” says Perlita Baula of the National Tobacco Administration. Traditional healers in the Philippines use tobacco leaves (38) to treat wounds and insect bites, researchers want to assess the plant's potential as a topical pain killer and antibacterial medicine. The plant also has a high protein (39) content which can be extracted and added to food supplements. The most promoting by-products are paper and particle board. “We're doing this to supplement the farmers' income,” notes Baula, “ (40) Although I don't think we will ever eliminate smoking through this program.”

Pembahasan

Teks di atas bisa kita lengkapi dengan kata-kata yang sesuai dengan konteks bacaan.

36. Crops artinya tanaman, sesuai konteks kata sebelumnya tobacco.

37. Find alternatives artinya menemukan alternatif sesuai konteks kalimat setelahnya tentang usaha mencari alternatif penggunaan tembakau.

38. To treat wounds artinya merawat luka, sesuai dengan konteks kata setelahnya yaitu wounds (luka).

39. Content artinya kandungan. sesuai dengan konteks kata sebelumnya yaitu high protein.

40. Although artinya meski pun, sesuai dengan konteks paragraf yang menceritakan sulitnya menghilangkan penggunaan tembakau.

21. What is the volume of the Philippine Sea​


Jawaban:

The Philippine Sea is a marginal sea of the Western Pacific Ocean east of the Philippine archipelago (hence the name), occupying an estimated surface area of 5 million square kilometers (2×106 sq mi).[1] The Philippine Sea Plate forms the floor of the sea.[2] It is bordered by the first island chain to the west, which comprises the Ryukyu Islands in the northwest and Taiwan in the due west, and the Filipino islands of Luzon, Catanduanes, Samar, Leyte and Mindanao to the southwest; by the Japanese islands of Honshu, Shikoku and Kyūshū to the north; by the second island chain to the east, which comprises the Bonin Islands and Iwo Jima in the northeast, the Mariana Islands (including Guam, Saipan and Tinian) in the due east, and Halmahera, Palau, Yap and Ulithi (of the Caroline Islands) in the southeast; and by Indonesia's Morotai Island to the south.[3]
